How It Works

ZKPassport reads the secure NFC chip in your government-issued passport and generates Noir-based zero-knowledge proofs that can verify specific attributes (like age, nationality, or simply that you're human) without revealing the underlying personal data.

Noir-Powered Privacy

Using the Noir zero-knowledge proof language, ZKPassport compiles privacy-preserving circuits that run directly on your mobile device. The Barretenberg proving system is optimized for ARM64 processors, ensuring fast proof generation on modern smartphones.

Use Cases

  • Age Verification: Prove you're over 18 without revealing your exact age or birthdate
  • Nationality Proof: Verify citizenship without exposing passport details
  • Humanity Verification: Prove you're a real person for social platforms and airdrops
  • Sybil Resistance: One person, one identity for voting and governance systems

Technical Architecture

  • Noir Circuits: Custom zero-knowledge circuits for identity verification
  • Barretenberg Backend: Mobile-optimized proving system
  • React Native: Cross-platform mobile app framework
  • Rust Integration: Native performance for cryptographic operations

Privacy Guarantees

Your personal data never leaves your device. The app generates mathematical proofs using Noir circuits that can be verified by others without revealing the source information. All proof generation happens client-side using WebAssembly compilation of Noir circuits.
